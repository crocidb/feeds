I love sandwiches. Earlier today I was thinking about how you can eat a sandwich at any time of the day. I have eaten sandwiches for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. When I saw Zachary’s “[Sandwich Questionnaire](https://zacharykai.net/notes/sandwiches)“, I thought *I have to respond to this!*

I have been thinking about responding to the coffee questionnaire on [Syl’s Blog](https://syls.blog/coffee/) but, for the first time in at least a year, I am taking a break from coffee! TL;DR: I started drinking a lot of tea over Christmas, and, slowly, I ended up replacing my tea habits with coffee. It has been a week since I had my last cup of coffee. Hence, while now may not be the right time to write about coffee, there is never a wrong time to write about sandwiches.

How many sandwiches do you eat per week?
----------

Approximately seven, one per day.

When do you eat sandwiches?
----------

I usually have a cheese toastie for lunch. If I’m out and about, I will try to get a baguette from Greggs, a bakery chain here in the UK (cheese and tomato salad is my favourite) or another sandwich.

What’s your go-to sandwich order?
----------

If I am going to buy a sandwich, I will look for either an egg and cress or a ploughman’s (cheese, pickle, lettuce), or the aforementioned baguette from Greggs. I don’t like sandwiches with too much cheese and prefer sandwiches without mayonnaise (with the exception of egg and cress).

At home, I like a cheese toastie, which is two slices of bread between which cheese is sandwiched, with the sandwich grilled. I also like toasting two slices of bread and then putting cheese in the middle after I have toasted the bread, especially for an evening snack. I will be calling this a “toasted cheese sandwich” (not to be confused with a cheese toastie; I’m aware the name is not semantically sound but I am unsure how else to refer to this, and I think the whimsical, alliterative term “semantically sound” is enough to atone for my semantic un-soundness in this matter).

What’s your favourite type of bread?
----------

I like all kinds of bread.

Do you like condiments?
----------

Not really. I have a thing about liquids in food. I do like hot sauce on a cheese toastie, though.

What about pickles?
----------

I *love* pickles.

Sweet or savoury?
----------

Savoury. With that said, I did really enjoy the limited-edition strawberry and cream sandwich sold by M&S shops across the UK last summer in celebration of the Wimbledon tennis tournament. The sandwich was a bit too sweet to have on a regular basis, but I certainly enjoyed it.

Do you cut your sandwiches?
----------

I like to cut cheese toasties diagonally (from corner to corner), and toasted cheese and all other sandwiches vertically (from side to side).

What’s the worst sandwich crime?
----------

Too many condiments.

What’s your ideal sandwich-eating environment?
----------

I love eating sandwiches anywhere. I like eating sandwiches on trains because those moments are usually preceded by a full day!

Why sandwiches?
----------

I have many fond memories of sandwiches. For example, when I was in primary school, I would get a sandwich in a bag that was sealed with a sticker. The stickers varied and so they were fun to collect. Me and some of my friends would put them in our lunchboxes. Technically in this story the sandwich was the means by which I got a sticker, but I will use this as an opportunity to affirm that the sandwich was significant in itself: a meal well-deserved after a morning of learning.
