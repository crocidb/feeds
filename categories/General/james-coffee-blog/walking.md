After reading – and enjoying a delicious coffee – I let my feet take me where they wanted to go. At the intersection of two roads on the Royal Mile – the heart of Old Town in Edinburgh – I turned my head right and thought about how long it had been since I had walked down the hill. The roads are narrow, but the views both along the way and at the destination are wonderful. There are many eye-catching cobblestone buildings to see, some with histories extending in the hundreds of years.

I sat down in the park at the bottom of the hill and watched the world go by for a bit. Soon thereafter, a troupe of pipers came past. I don’t think there is another city in the world where bagpipes are played so commonly. I love the sound of the bagpipes. Whether the song being played is one I have heard a hundred times before or never heard, I am always delighted. I sat for a while enjoying the music, then continued walking around.

Walking on paths I hadn’t followed in a while, I saw the city from new perspectives. I looked up to buildings from lower vantage points, and felt in awe of their grandeur and stonework. I enjoyed the first signs of autumn in the trees. I meandered. By chance, a building I have wanted to visit before but has always been closed was open so I was able to go in and take a look.

Earlier this week I read a blog post in which someone quoted Winnie the Pooh, who said “I always get to where I am going by walking away from where I have been.” These words have been on my mind all week.

I saw today as I did because I let my feet take me where they wanted to go. I took in all of the sights and sounds of Edinburgh in the beginning of autumn. *The trees are going to change more,* I thought, looking forward to see how colourful the city will be in the coming weeks.

Walking back up the Royal Mile, I saw a quote carved in stone in the Parliament building, from Kathleen Jamie’s *The Storm, The Bonniest Companie*, which I wrote down so as not to forget. The words read “*Be brave. By the weird-song in the dark you'll find your way*”.
