I have been thinking about hand-writing and websites in the background for a little while.

I made a hand-written font using [Calligraphr](https://www.calligraphr.com/) a few months ago. I enjoyed making this font, but haven't used it for a web project yet. Here's what it looks like:

![](https://editor.jamesg.blog/content/images/2026/02/handwriting.png)

While making the font, I experimented with putting love hearts above the `i` characters. The pen I used was perhaps too thick to add all the details I wanted – the font presented above is in a regular weight, although it looks bold. With that said, I do like the direction of the love hearts. I wonder what other character I could add to a font.

I got thinking about hand-written websites again after seeing [Coffee Receipt Stories](https://coffeereceiptstories.com), a gallery of stories written on the backs of coffee receipts. I love the detail on that website.

This inspired me to try my hand at bringing another hand-written idea I have had to life: creating a grid of icons which could point to various pages on my website.

Here is the grid of icons I made:

![](https://editor.jamesg.blog/content/images/2026/02/drawing.png)

There are a few details in here. The front cover of the textbook was (my best attempt) at drawing one of my university textbooks. The WWW design is from an early [World Wide Web logo by Robert Cailliau](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/WWW_logo_by_Robert_Cailliau.svg/250px-WWW_logo_by_Robert_Cailliau.svg.png). The label is "Web (ours)" because [the web is for everyone](https://www.panmacmillan.com/authors/tim-berners-lee/this-is-for-everyone/9781035023677).

"Moments of Joy" is depicted with balloons because, as Winnie the Pooh says, "Nobody can be uncheered with a balloon." The "About Me" section depicts various objects related to my interests. The shape in the bottom-right corner is supposed to be a [pet rock](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1FVF-9KQiPo). [1]

I had fun making both of these things and playing around with adding something hand-written to the web. The latter grid of icons also gave me an excuse to use the scanner in my printer.
