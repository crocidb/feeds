[Lou](https://louplummer.lol/nice-stranger/) wrote a blog post asking the question “What is the nicest thing a stranger has ever done for you?” I read his blog post and then spent some time thinking about how I would answer. One moment comes to mind.

When I was in my late teens, I had gone on a journey to the city which involved a bus ride and a train. The bus ride was a significant leg of the journey. On my way back home, I took the step up onto the bus, then, if I recall correctly, realised that I needed cash to pay for the trip instead of my card. I searched my pockets. I didn’t have enough cash. I was two pounds short. I froze and was a bit confused. *What do I do?* I could go to a cash machine and get money, but then I would be home later.

An older woman behind me in the queue – perhaps in her 60s or 70s – stepped in and paid the two pounds I needed to cover my journey. I was so grateful. I remember where I sat on the bus, near the front, and how my gratitude stayed with me both for the whole journey home and indeed until now. Knowing I would likely never meet the woman again, half-way through the bus ride I asked her if there was a charity to which I could donate two pounds on her behalf. She said it was alright. I think I made a donation to a charity anyway, but I can’t quite remember.

The bus driver may have been kind enough to let me on the bus anyway, realising I was young and that I looked confused. But that was far from certain. I never took for granted how much it meant for the person behind me in the queue to cover the two pounds I needed to pay for my trip. I hope she is doing well!
