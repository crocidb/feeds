For the last [two](https://jamesg.blog/2024/12/05/spotify-wrapped-2024) [years](https://jamesg.blog/2023/11/29/spotify-wrapped), I have posted my Spotify Wrapped updates to my website. I think they serve as much as a reminder to me about what music I was listening to as being potentially interesting to you (I hope, maybe?). This year I moved to Apple Music part-way through the year so my update is a bit fragmented but, as with the last two years, Taylor Swift came up on top of my recap.

I was particularly proud of the “Top Artists by Month” in which, in eight of the 11 months before December 2025 I listened to Taylor Swift more than any other artist:

![A table that shows my listening to Taylor Swift more than any other artist in March, April, May, June, August, October, and November this year.](https://editor.jamesg.blog/content/images/2025/12/IMG_3610-Large.jpeg)

I also listened to a lot of music by Bon Iver (his latest album, Sable Fable, is wonderful), Lorde (so many great albums!), and a bit from Florence + the Machine (so good!), Death Cab for Cutie (great for long train rides!) and Oh Wonder (their album 22 Make is my go-to right now, especially the song *Stop Waiting*).

One band I have listened to throughout the year is The Colourist, a Los Angeles-based band who delightfully and purposefully chose to add a “u” to their name for reasons I can’t remember. Their music always brings me energy. The band broke up ten years ago or so and so I think my hopes for another album are somewhat dashed, but nonetheless I am so glad they wrote all the songs they did! *Little Games* is a good introduction to their music.

My top five songs were by number of plays this year across all artists were:

* The Fate of Ophelia by Taylor Swift
* Opalite by Taylor Swift
* Team by Lorde
* Mimi by Big Red Machine
* Cardigan by Taylor Swift

There is one part of The Fate of Ophelia that stays in my memory more than any other:

> All that time  
> I sat alone in my tower  
> …  
> Saved my heart from the fate of  
> Ophelia

I can play The Fate of Ophelia on piano; it is among my favourite songs to play right now.
