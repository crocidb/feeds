I have been waking up with anticipation every morning recently. I say “good morning, world”, and then anxiously peek through the edge of the curtains. I hope for the blue sky.

This last week has been rainy and grey. I tried to understand why but the meteorology went over my head. What I did learn is that it wasn’t just me who seen the clouds: the weather has generally been more grey than normal, and we know why (or, at least, the meteorologists do).

The clouds have been unrelenting.

This morning, like all mornings this past week, I had hope. Maybe today the clouds will recede. I saw an orange colour in the sky, one I haven’t seen in at least a week. I saw more blue. I thought about the juxtaposition between the blue patches and the dark clouds that still loomed. When will the clouds go away?

My morning walk today was refreshing. I could hardly wait to get outside, knowing that the clouds may soon be back. I had been waiting to see the blue sky for so long. Here it was. In small patches, yes, but nonetheless wonderful. The birds sang with grace and volume, as if they, too, were filled with enthusiasm for the new day.

As I write, a few hours after waking up, the clouds are back. But I know that the clouds will recede and spring will one day come; the grey of winter will soon be a memory, drained out by the new day.
