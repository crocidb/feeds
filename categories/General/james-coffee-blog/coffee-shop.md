I read The Frugal Gamer’s post “[If you were going to open up a shop, what would you sell?](https://www.thefrugalgamer.net/blog/2025/12/16/if-you-were-going-to-open-up-a-shop-what-would-you-sell/)” with great joy. Like The Frugal Gamer, I too am bundled up inside bracing the weather. I was hoping to go for a walk but the rain and wind has convinced me to stay inside and instead huddle around a warm beverage. Which brings me to my answer.

If I were to open a shop, I think it would be a coffee shop.

I have spent so much time in coffee shops and tea houses. When I was young, I used to go to cafes with my grandparents. I looked forward to the moments when I could graduate from drinking juice to tea (although juice was amazing!). I joined them in eating scones. All of those memories make me happy.

When I got older, I started going to coffee shops. There was an independent coffee shop near to where I grew up which was especially nice. The shop, down a quiet side street, sold second-hand books, rugs, and, of course, tea and coffee. They had a particularly nice selection of cakes. I haven't been in a few years, but I definitely want to go back. There is a particular cake I'd like to try!

I love coffee shops because they are a place I can be in public but also rest. I can sit and read a book, as I often do. I can enjoy a cup of coffee. I can listen to the world around me and notice things. I will listen to the music on the loudspeaker, revelling every time a song comes on that I like. I can watch the world go by. I might even write a few words in my notebook. The description “third place” definitely describes how I feel about coffee shops – they are a place between everything else, of rest, of conversation, of ideas.

I think my coffee shop would have a selection of books that you could read while drinking your coffee, sort of like a miniature library. Maybe some books would be for sale. I would probably sell coffee beans, and nice cakes. Really, the sales part doesn’t come to mind as much as the atmosphere does. I’d want to create a place that is warm and cosy; quiet but also where you can meet new friends.

The shop might be quite small, such that there is a natural closeness in the environment. I would probably want to make coffee by hand. I’d want to create a place where you can make new friends if you want – where the anxious feel welcome – but also where you can sit by yourself and just listen to the world around you.

One shop that inspires me is the [Peepuru kissaten](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zOCejq92pdo). I have never been, but the linked video portrays a place that is warm and exciting and the hub of a community.

Whether or not I’ll open a coffee shop one day, I’m not sure. But, no matter what, their warmth will stay with me – indeed, the warmth of a coffee shop exists both in the moment you are there, in the anticipation of being there, and in the moments after when you leave with a newfound energy that comes from resting in a pleasant atmosphere.
