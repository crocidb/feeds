This month’s [IndieWeb Book Club](https://indieweb.org/IndieWeb_Book_Club) book, the [Non-Designer’s Design Book by Robin Williams](https://openlibrary.org/books/OL8667671M/The_Non-Designer's_Design_Book), felt like it was written for me. One of my goals this year was to learn more about design, so I was delighted to see this book on the list.

Through the book, Williams introduces and details four principles to keep in mind when doing graphic or digital design: Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity. There is a handy, memorable abbreviation to remember these which I will leave as an exercise to the reader.

I appreciate that Williams included visual examples throughout the book that demonstrate each principle; the graphics and words accompany each other well. I felt like the images were not just examples, but something that could be studied through the lens both of the chapter in which they were featured as well as the preceding chapters. *Oh, there’s contrast being used here! Oh, I like the way this is aligned!* were thoughts that came through my head.

One interesting-to-me take away was how nice it is when you commit to either a left or right alignment for text, and how uniform a design can look when you stick to a given alignment. I thought about how this relates to my blog. I realised the idea of “trapped white space”, introduced in the same chapter on alignment, may be why I prefer my sidebar when it is aligned to the right as it is now rather than the left.

If you are interested in learning about design, I highly recommend the Non-Designer’s Design Book. Thank you to [Al](https://alabut.com/writing/) for proposing this as the book for the month. You can learn more about the book club, and propose a book for a future month, on the [IndieWeb Book Club wiki page](https://indieweb.org/IndieWeb_Book_Club).
