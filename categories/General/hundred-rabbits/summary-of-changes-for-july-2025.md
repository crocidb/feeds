Hey everyone!

This is the list of all the changes we've done to our projects during the month of July.

<img src="../media/content/videos/july_2025.jpg" alt="a hummingbird and a kingfisher who both are claiming Pino as theirs in Solresol." loading="lazy" width="1200" height="679">

* **[Catjam 2025](https://itch.io/jam/catjam-2025)**, finished a small game called [Polycat](https://hundredrabbits.itch.io/polycat)!
* **100r.ca**, added [Vancouver Island West Coast](https://100r.ca/site/vancouver_island_west_coast.html), [Ucluelet](https://100r.ca/site/ucluelet.html), [Port San Juan](https://100r.ca/site/port_san_juan.html), [Juan de Fuca Strait](https://100r.ca/site/juan_de_fuca_strait.html) and [Cadboro Bay](https://100r.ca/site/cadboro_bay.html).
* **[Theme Editor](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/theme)**, made a nicer theme editor for our tools ecosystem.
* **[Punk Rabbits](https://kokorobot.ca/site/punk_rabbits.html)**, started a drawing series of rabbit characters with the goal of making one hundred.

This month we sailed into the Juan de Fuca Strait, escorted by pods of orcas and a lone young humpback whale. The last time we had been here was in July 2020, on our way back from Japan. After weathering gale force winds at anchor in [Becher Bay](https://100r.ca/media/content/travel/becher_bay.jpg), we stopped in Port San Juan. The beaches there are covered in old growth driftwood, carried there by winter storms. Some trees are so large and have been there so long that people have installed [swings](https://100r.ca/media/content/travel/port_san_juan_05.jpg) on them. The way to Barkley Sound was bleak, we spent 10 hours of it in a fog bank, only to emerge near Cape Beale to a bright sun over jade-colored waters. We pulled into Ucluelet the next day, just in time to meet up with our friend Avi to view the building site for their [upcoming boatyard project](https://avibryant.com/2025).

We spent a few days anchored in Barkley Sound, in an anchorage with the biggest population of [hummingbirds](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2025/07/10.html) we'd ever seen. The hummingbird visits were constant, with 3-4 buzzing around us at all times. During our stay there we completed our game entry for [Catjam](https://itch.io/jam/catjam-2025) named [Polycat](https://hundredrabbits.itch.io/polycat). The game is very hard, but also very short. Watch a [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kzH6GchYMNM) of Devine playing the game.

In the second half of July, Pino sailed back to the Southern Gulf islands and stayed anchored alongside a friend, messing with their [laser engraver](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2025/08/01.html), hiking, picking blackberries, and working on projects. Instead of hummingbirds, in Fulford, we had kingfishers, they really liked sitting on the wind vane's arrow on top of the mast. See this [amazing drone footage](https://merveilles.town/@neauoire/114954691829294465) shot by our friends aboard MV Poem.

Devine has been invited to talk about permacomputing at [Critical Signals](https://criticalsignals.nz/programme/permacomputing-101/) on August 12th. They will try to introduce some of the ideas that they find most interesting via practical examples. Save the date!

**Book Club:** This month we are reading *Ancillary Justice* by Ann Leckie.
