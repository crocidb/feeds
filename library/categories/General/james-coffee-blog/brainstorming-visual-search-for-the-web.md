When I am chatting about a website with friends, sometimes I forget the name of the website. I remember what the site is about, and often remember visual details about the site, too. A few months ago, I described a website that aggregated blogs and also mentioned it had a yellow background. I could remember the purpose of the site and its colour and rough layout, but not the name. (The site was [Minifeed](https://minifeed.net/welcome); I am in awe of the person who found the website based on my description.)

Since then I have occasionally thought about what a visual search engine would look like for the web. What if I could search for friends’ websites based on a descriptor like “serif fonts” or “yellow background” or “sidebar layout”? This may aid in retrieval such as in the scenario outlined in the previous paragraph, but it would also allow for exploration by theme. What if I could type “website with a 2000s Frutiger aero theme” and find a result? Or “website with a cool laptop layout”?

While the ability to search with natural language would be interesting, retrieval is only one piece of exploring the web by aesthetic. I think tags would be an equally interesting avenue to explore – a [folksonomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folksonomy) of website aesthetics could be built. With tags, you could create a place to look for inspiration where users can explore tags that have already been created or search for tags related to their ideas. Users could peruse the list of tags, choose a direction, and keep exploring.

Two projects of great inspiration are the [Web Design Museum](https://www.webdesignmuseum.org/) which document website styles by era for various large websites, and the [Consumer Aesthetics Research Institute (CARI)](https://cari.institute/) which have curated different aesthetics. Both of these sites have created taxonomies to navigate their websites.

If anything like this exists, please let me know! Otherwise, I would love to see more explorations toward this idea!

*(By extension of the natural language visual search idea, but separately, what if could search all of the myriad screenshots I have taken of my website and have on my desktop? I’m actually looking for a specific screenshot right now. I can describe it, but it will take me a while to find. A local search engine would be of great aid!)*
