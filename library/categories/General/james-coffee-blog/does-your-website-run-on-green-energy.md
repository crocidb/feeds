**Update, 7 February 2026:** A reader reached out with an argument that made me reconsider my perspective on this topic. Using a data centre that runs on green energy would be most meaningful when the grid that fuels the energy is running fully on renewables, so that use of the data centre does not use up energy in such a way that other energy users have to rely on non-renewable energy.

I still think it is worth taking a look to see what environmental commitments your web host has made, but there is so much you can do additionally to reduce emissions whose impact is both meaningful and unambiguous. I recommend the UN's "[Actions for a healthy planet](https://www.un.org/en/actnow/ten-actions)" guidance for things you can do today to reduce your carbon emissions.

I am leaving my original post below for future reference.

<details> <summary>The original post</summary>

A few weeks ago I did a day-long training [Carbon Literacy Project training course](https://carbonliteracy.com/) through my university. When I was taking the course and considering ways I can reduce my carbon footprint, I started thinking about technology. I don’t know much about calculating emissions from technology (and as I understand this is a complex field) but, at the very least, I wanted to check whether my web host runs green energy.

I use Hetzner to host my website and web services, who use [100% hydropower to power their data centres](https://www.hetzner.com/unternehmen/nachhaltigkeit/). I also use Cloudflare for DNS, who report using [100% renewable energy in their 2025 Impact Report](https://cf-assets.www.cloudflare.com/slt3lc6tev37/5uWkeoLzbsUs5YNNKY4rTp/5765c603d3b5e0b7896401d0360bd84f/Impact-Report-2025_v2.pdf).

After doing this research, I got to thinking: I should probably write this down to encourage others to check to see whether their web hosts use green energy, too. If you have a few minutes, do a bit of research to see if the place you host your website uses green energy. If it doesn’t, consider whether there is a green host you could use for future projects, or whether if you ever want to or need to migrate you could look at a green host.
