Over on Bear Blog, Thereabouts posted “[The greatest breakfast food in existence](https://thereabouts.bearblog.dev/the-greatest-breakfast-food-in-existence/)”. When I read the title, my face lit up. “A blog post about breakfast food!” It got me thinking about what breakfast brings me the most joy. I think the answer is waffles and coffee.

This time around two years ago, The Tortured Poets Department (TTPD) had just been released and I was on a trip to the East Coast of the United States. I still remember the breakfasts: Waffles (almost) every day, purchased from a diner. [1]

I love the ambiance of diners: staff attentively placing menus in front of customers who walk in and take a seat at the bar, people sitting side by side, the sound of conversation in the kitchen, coffee poured from a thermos, a television with closed captions on in the background. As much as I love speciality coffee, diner coffee has a special place in my heart.

Diners make delicious waffles. My favourite order is a Belgian waffle with fruit. I like the fruit to be on the side so that I can put it on the waffle myself. A mixture of melon and orange and grapes and berries or really any fruit makes a great topping. [2] And if there is conversation in the background? I am doubly happy.

I like making fresh waffles but only with other people. There is a lot of clean-up work to do and it’s more fun to clean up with someone (and it means I can delegate the duty of cracking the egg which I don’t enjoy; this will be necessary until I finally do some research on how to make vegan waffles).

Waffles are a treat: I don’t have them very often. This likely contributes to how much joy they bring; sometimes things are more special because they don’t come along every day.
