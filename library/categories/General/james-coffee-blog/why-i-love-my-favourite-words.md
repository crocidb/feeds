Every so often, I use the word *delightful* in conversation. *That is delightful!* I try not to use “delightful” too often, but I do like to bring it into a discussion every now and again. It is one of my favourite words.

The word *delightful*, like the word *awe*, makes me feel a certain way when I think about it. *That’s delightful!* feels like it embodies more energy than “that is great!” I try my best to bring positive energy to a conversation where appropriate. I love to make people smile and want to bring little moments of joy into someone’s day. Maybe “delightful” can bring a little bit of joy.

The word *delightful* brings me joy; maybe, by using that word, I can share how I feel with someone else. Indeed, the words you choose to express a thought mean something, and can themselves both communicate and evoke a feeling.

I have more words that I love, too. When I think of the word *awe*, I think of Nature. The hills of home come to mind, so vast and green on a summer’s day that it is almost impossible not to say *wow*. I think of the colourful sunrises and sunsets that have welcomed and marked the end of each day. I think of the Romantic period in art. All of these thoughts come to mind from one word: awe.

I like the word *oratory*. It makes me think of speeches that change the world. I like the word *tactile* because it makes me think of the sense of touch. I like the word *hope* because it keeps me going. I like the word *joy* because it brings light to the day. I like the word *colour* because it makes me think of Impressionism, my favourite genre of art, and makes me think about how colours make us feel. I like the word *wonder* because it makes me wonder, and may encourage others to wonder too. I like the word *coffee* because it reminds me of both a good average and all of the interactions that can happen over coffee.

I could go on for a long time thinking about words and why I love them. What is in common between all the words I love is that they sound good when spoken, feel good when said, and have a meaning, at least to me.
