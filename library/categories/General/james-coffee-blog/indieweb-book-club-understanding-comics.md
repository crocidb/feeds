The [IndieWeb Book Club](https://indieweb.org/IndieWeb_Book_Club) choice for November 2025 was Scott McCloud’s *Understanding Comics*. I wanted to write a post about this book, but I have lately found myself spending a lot of time reading books rather than writing. My time recently has reminded me of how much I love reading, especially fiction and manga.

Understanding Comics is a book about comics written as a comic, a delightful choice that let McCloud not only talk about comics but show off techniques used in comics as he introduced concepts. One that stuck out to me was the idea of what happens between the panels – in the so-called “gutter” between two images in a comic.

A lot can happen in that space between, and it is up to the reader to imagine exactly what might happen. The comic artist in this way acts as a guide – you see two panels and you can have a bit of fun thinking about what happens in the middle.

This has me thinking about between spaces more generally. I read something last week about how much happens in conference “hallways” – in the space between talks where you can chat with and meet new people. I then started to think about Sir. Tim Berners-Lee noting how the space between offices in CERN – a hallway, too, or, if I remember correctly, the intersection of two hallways – was a space where people could chat.

Perhaps the idea of “spaces between” relates to the topic of distance in writing that I have been thinking a bit about, at least to some extent. The gutter creates distance between two moments, if only a tiny amount. The space between panels is a separator, like a space between words in an essay. In a manga I am reading right now, I have noticed there can be more space between panels to mark a new scene (usually also signified by a panel that indicates a new scene, like a skyline in a panel or progression of skylines between panels, or a panel dedicated to a specific building in which the next scene will be set).

I thoroughly enjoyed reading Understanding Comics and would recommend it to anyone interested in learning more about the art of making comics!
