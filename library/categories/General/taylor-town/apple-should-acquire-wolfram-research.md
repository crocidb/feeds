[Wolfram Research](https://www.wolfram.com) is a strange company. Its CEO [livestreams their R&D meetings](https://livestreams.stephenwolfram.com/category/live-ceoing/) and is publicly pursuing [a theory of everything](https://www.wolframphysics.org). Maybe next he'll solve [the Navier-Stokes smoothness problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Navier–Stokes_existence_and_smoothness) and use it to attenuate his [reality-distortion field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reality_distortion_field).

But in all seriousness, Wolfram makes [incredible software](https://demonstrations.wolfram.com). Mathematica has been impressive [since 1989](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reality_distortion_field). Wolfram Language blew my mind [in 2010](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EjCWdsrVcBM), and continues to drop [massive updates](https://www.wolfram.com/mathematica/quick-revision-history/).

More than any other company, Wolfram was perfectly poised to capitalize on the generative AI revolution. [Their newest LLM updates](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u4CRHtjyHTI) are jaw-dropping.

And yet, Mathematica remains an academic curiosity. Industry software devs rarely dabble in Wolfram world. I myself haven't been able to justify forking over [$399/year](https://www.wolfram.com/mathematica/pricing/home-hobby/) for it, despite having a pile of real-world use-cases for it.

Meanwhile, Google and Microsoft's OpenAI are commoditizing intelligence. Apple is, uh, sitting on Siri.

**Apple should acquire Wolfram Research.**

It would be good for Wolfram:

* Many devs are hesitant to use Wolfram's opaque software. Apple could afford to make everything open-source.
* Apple has limitless computing resources. All of Wolfram Cloud would probably be a footnote on the iCloud servers.
* Wolfram apps could severely benefit from Apple's intuitive UX teams.
* It seems like more devs choose APL than Wolfram Language for [Advent of Code](https://adventofcode.com) each year. Apple's acquisition would probably cause unprecedented hype, and could foster a thriving community.

It would be good for Apple:

* Mathematica could become a "killer app" for Apple devices. There's nothing else remotely like Wolfram's ecosystem.
* Apple has always had a soft spot for schools. Paired with LLMs, a [free/cheap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loss_leader) Mathematica could revolutionize education.
* A deeper integration with Wolfram's knowledge base could make Siri smarter.
* Mathematica would be poetic justice for how Apple's [graphing calculator](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dl643JFJWig) saved the transition to PowerPC.
* [iOS development with Wolfram Language](https://www.wolframcloud.com/objects/c9576832-bd9a-4dd8-a9ed-7e8cccdf2b9a) could be pretty neat.
* Wolfram Language is great for programmatically editing [images](https://reference.wolfram.com/language/guide/ImageProcessing.html)/[videos](https://reference.wolfram.com/language/guide/VideoProcessing.html)/[audio](https://reference.wolfram.com/language/guide/AudioProcessing.html), and could make automations with [Siri shortcuts](https://support.apple.com/en-au/guide/shortcuts/welcome/ios) infinitely more useful.
* Functions from Wolfram Language could give Siri thousands of useful verbs to enact: "Siri, [add some reverb to this recording](https://reference.wolfram.com/language/ref/AudioReverb.html)." "Siri, [make this photo look like this other one](https://www.wolfram.com/language/12/machine-learning-for-images/built-in-image-style-transfer.html?product=language)." "Siri, [trim this video](https://reference.wolfram.com/language/ref/VideoTrim.html)." And so on.
* [All the other symbolic functions](https://reference.wolfram.com/language/) would make excellent additions to Numbers, Pages, Logic Pro, and the rest of Apple's first-party apps.
* Mathematica would be a lovely addition to the Apple Vision Pro. Stereoscopic vision and 3D manipulation would make certain branches of research much more pleasant.
