Most of my essay/project ideas are stored in a file called `ideas.txt`. It's 897KB of [plain-text](https://taylor.town/long-live-plain-text).

101,721 words of cryptic nonsense like this:

```
water under the fridge
"todo bankrupty"
finishing list of favorite things
extremely low-effort children's books
pocket gods
common ways to waste your life
"microwaves are for cowards"
death requires no prep; there's nothing you can do
"the view from inside my skull"
“stuff that happens to other people": death, theft, pain, addiction, victory
"metaphysics was invented by papyrus manufacturerers to sell more scrolls"
a guide to giving trinkets to strangers
exploring the word warriors underused word lists
moon's haunted
rootin' tootin' pollutants
```

Each concept makes complete sense to me.

I see *microwaves are for cowards* and think "Oh yeah, [Tyler Cowen](https://marginalrevolution.com) and my cousin Brooke both oppose microwave ovens for different reasons. Are their reasons sound? Are there other people like this? That reminds me of people who brag about not owning televisions. Do I act like one of those people, even though I own a televison? How did stereotypes of television and microwave owners evolve over time?"

And so starts a search for [secret artifacts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artifact_(archaeology)). Hunt. Dig for truth. Filter noise. Appraise opinions. Label, organize, and collect dust.

My brain feels like 31 seasons of [*Hoarders*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hoarders_(TV_series)). Thousands of jars of fermenting thoughts line my skull's walls. Some ideas spoiled long ago, but it's hard to tell kimchi from poison until I pop a lid.

To clear mountains of [curios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bric-à-brac) from my mind, I mix them into essays, stories, software, songs, dolls, carvings, etc.

Distillation is difficult. My compression process is [lossy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lossy_compression) and full of [artifacts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compression_artifact). Sometimes I'm ashamed of the artifacts; sometimes it's the whole point. [Fermentation funk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SCOBY) is weird.

I have no idea what I'm doing, but I'm happy doing lots of it.
