In [American football](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_NfnXdXpjL0), the [line of scrimmage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Line_of_scrimmage) is an imaginary border at which the next round of play begins.

To "move the ball forward" is to push that imaginary border into favorable territory.

We fantasize about heroic [Hail Mary passes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hail_Mary_pass), but often fumble when it counts.

Creation is a toilsome, boring, everyday thing. Historic plays can't be planned in advance.

Keep moving that line of scrimmage forward. Use your lunchbreak to add a paragraph to your magnum opus. [Scrounge for between time.](https://taylor.town/between-time) Clearly structure large projects with lots of alcoves that can be decorated independently. Build [Hemingway Bridges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iceberg_theory) that beg to be completed. Do something. Anything.
