The Year of Trailblazing 2022-2023
----------

### End of the Trail 2023.08.08 ###

I charted a wild path over the last two-ish years:

* finally started antidepressants
* became a dad
* quit booze and cigarettes after ten years of attempts
* conquered my internet addiction
* started a popularish blog
* biked from SF to LA
* [failed at starting a tech conference](https://taylor.town/outland-wasnt-ready)
* botched a startup launch
* read [a ton of books](https://taylor.town/books)
* overcame chronic crippling anxiety

Things I learned:

* don't think about yourself while others are talking
* quitting booze was a decision I needed to make years ago, but it's never too late to start living
* track how you spend your time until you're happy with your life
* friends and family might be overrated
* be obsessive but always come back to earth after a few weeks
* limit the number of queues and inputs into your mind
* make mayhem
* frequently finish small things
* don't waste brainpower on impractical knowledge and world news and things you can't control -- learn things only to make things

### Keeping the Path 2023.01.03 ###

I want to explore! I want to ascend! I want to yearn and struggle and grow!

In 2023, I'll be pursuing some lofty goals:

* publish a novella
* go on a camping trip
* become a best dad
* launch a startup
* organize a tech conference
* benchpress my own bodyweight
* continue [my sobriety experiment](https://taylor.town/8-weekends)
* release a VR "game"
* [cycle from San Francisco to Los Angeles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AIDS/LifeCycle)

Blazing these trails will require skills that I don't *yet* possess:

* discipline
* finishing projects I'm disinterested in
* managing fear and anxiety
* consistency

Let's go!

### (Officially) Blazing the Trail 2022.10.17 ###

When I decided to start my "year of trailblazing" earlier this year, I was speedrunning major life changes:

* purchased my first home
* moved to an unfamiliar climate
* started a new job
* changed my name
* had my first child
* [quit drinking forever](https://taylor.town/1000-weekends)

For me, charting unexplored territory has been a difficult delight! My soul has been fully engaged.

But the year's not over yet! Continuing into 2023, I'll still be trailblazing (literally and figuratively):

* weekly hikes
* seasonal camping
* [cycling from San Francisco to Los Angeles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AIDS/LifeCycle)
* launching my first real startup
* starting a tech conference
