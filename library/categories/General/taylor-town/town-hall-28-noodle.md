Hey! It's been a while. I'm alive. Life is good.

 If you want more frequent updates, consider following me on [RSS](https://taylor.town/feed.xml), [X](https://x.com/surprisetalk), [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/profile/taylor.town), and [Mastodon](https://mastodon.social/@surprisetalk).

I moved to Seattle earlier this year! It's a wonderful (and severely underrated) city. So much moss! Great people, great vibes, great weather, great culture, great food, and great parks. Let me know if you're ever in the area. Let's hang!

Anyway, I recently read [*Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned: The Myth of the Objective*](https://bookshop.org/p/books/why-greatness-cannot-be-planned-the-myth-of-the-objective-joel-lehman/349348ba97f93aa4?ean=9783319155234&next=t). It's a dull book that started a tiny circus in my skull. Its central argument is something like "objectives are counterproductive for solving many creative problems". Suddenly, so many of giant dreams/goals/plans seem silly. I've given myself permission to [noodle](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/noodle#american-noodle-3-verb-intransitive) again. Less pressure. It feels good.

Let this be a little reminder that you may noodle too.
