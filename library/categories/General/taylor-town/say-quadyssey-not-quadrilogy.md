"Trilogy" is a delightful word.

["Quadrilogy"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetralogy#Other_information) is a blight of the tongue.

["Tetralogy"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetralogy) is clunky. ["Quartet"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetralogy#Other_information) is broad.

I propose the following word:

>
>
> **quad•ys•sey** *(kwŏd′ĭ-sē) noun*
>
>
>
> *a work of art composed of four distinct works*
>
>

[![divergent](https://taylor.town/quadyssey.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divergent_(book_series))

Notable Quadysseys
----------

Quadysseys seem to be rare.

Please [email me](mailto:hello@taylor.town) if you can vouch for good candidate series.

* [Hyperion Cantos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperion_Cantos)
* [The Inheritance Cycle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Inheritance_Cycle)
* [Long Price Quartet](https://www.tor.com/2011/04/19/fantasy-for-grown-ups-daniel-abrahams-long-price-quartet/)

Plus Ones
----------

Adding a sequel or prequel to a trilogy does not make it a quadyssey.

* [Divergent + Four](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divergent_(book_series))
* [Lord of the Rings + The Hobbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings)
* [The Matrix + Resurrections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Matrix_(franchise))

Polygies
----------

Furthermore, I would change the rest of [wiktionary's trilogy companions](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tetralogy):

* duology
* trilogy
* quadyssey ~~quadrilogy~~
* polygy ~~pentalogy~~
* polygy ~~hexalogy~~
* polygy ~~heptalogy~~
* polygy ~~octalogy~~
* polygy ~~ennealogy~~
* polygy ~~decalogy~~
* polygy ~~polylogy~~

For example, [Harry Potter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Potter) is a 7-part polygy and [A Series of Unfortunate Events](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Series_of_Unfortunate_Events) is a 13-part polygy.
