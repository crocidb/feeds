Look at me! I'm suddenly satirical! Read my hot-takes on [death](https://taylor.town/to-your-grave) and [personal information](https://taylor.town/please-sell-my-personal-information) and [brilliant ideas](https://taylor.town/brilliant-app-idea).

After my cynicism dwindles, I tend to spend hours watching minutes slip by. Time is [scrounged](https://taylor.town/between-time) and [squandered](https://taylor.town/waiting-mode). Time is [shorter than you expect](https://taylor.town/500-hours) and [longer than you think](https://taylor.town/zeroes).

You [become what you do](https://taylor.town/pick-practical-principles). You can [multitask](https://taylor.town/busy-body) and [monotask](https://taylor.town/monotasking). You can make living [impossible](https://taylor.town/nothing-fulfills) or [inevitable](https://taylor.town/make-important-things-inevitable).

Smartphones are antithetical to action. [Give your phone a home](https://taylor.town/phone-home) and [leave your phone at home](https://taylor.town/leave-your-phone-at-home). Also [avoid leaving your home](https://taylor.town/decline-invitations) in general.

To see Outside simply, learn Toki Pona! It's can be used as a delightful baby sign-language. Check out my [manifesto](https://taylor.town/tpbsl-manifesto), [guide](https://taylor.town/tpbsl-guide), and [flash cards](https://taylor.town/tpbsl-flash-cards).

Next month, I'll dedicate [Between Time](https://taylor.town/between-time) to demolishing tech-debt at work. I fully intend to embark on "strategic" YOLO rewrites.

### Books ###

* ★★★★★ ***The Handmaid's Tale* by Margaret Atwood:** Much of *The Handmaid's Tale* is surely beyond my comprehension, but it provided a sobering glimpse into some of the unfairnesses of nature and society. Atwood's general take on United States fundamentalism is outstanding. The book is riveting throughout, but its ending secures it a "modern masterpiece".
* ★★★★☆ ***Animal Farm* by George Orwell:** *Animal Farm* delightfully illustrates how authoritarian regimes usurp control. Although the book seemed to be specifically a critique of Stalin's Russia, there are gems to glean about perverse institutional incentives.
* ★★★★☆ ***The Colour of Magic* (Discworld #1) by Terry Pratchett**: This was my first Terry Pratchett book. Oh boy, what a delight! If you like clever authors like Raymond Smullyan and Douglas Adams, you'll adore his writing. *The Colour of Magic* covers some surprisingly deep ideas about science and religion wearing a witty grin.
