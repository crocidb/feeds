The joke wasn't always about a suicidal bird.

[By 1847](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Why_did_the_chicken_cross_the_road?#cite_note-1), it had become a well-known [anti-joke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-humor). Instead of a typical [punchline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punch_line), the chicken joke purposefully delivers a disappointing resolution.

In 1899, [Henry H. Bliss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_of_Henry_H._Bliss) was struck by a taxicab -- the first recorded car casualty in the US.

 Before cars, people used vehicles called "horses". The non-LIDAR autopilot was ahead of its time, but the market was never going to accept 1HP and a 30-mile range. This is why Americans distrust European imports.

![US motor vehicle deaths per year](https://taylor.town/United_States_Motor_Vehicle_Deaths_per_Year.webp)

The US made more cars, faster cars, heavier cars; one could maim/kill oneself by stepping into oncoming traffic. All high-speed roads became portals to The Other Side.

 Songs like [Hit Me With Your Car](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmltUzDjKgA) and [Crash the Car](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GZlVT8gPGEs) derive humor from such safety violations.

Any arbitrary animal could've crossed the road, but we coincidentally chose the "chicken" -- an enduring symbol of cowardice.

In [games of chicken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicken_(game)), players demonstrate courage by yielding later than other players. Game theorists model such [payoff matrices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Normal-form_game) like this:

|      |Swerve | Stay  |
|------|-------|-------|
|Swerve| 0, 0  |\-1, +1|
| Stay |\+1, -1|\-∞, -∞|

This game normally has three Nash equilibria (i.e. stable strategies): (1) always chicken, (2) never chicken, or (3) match your opponent's stochastic strategy.

But not all chicken games are symmetric. When a lone man challenges armored vehicles, it feels callous to cram life into a lopsided payoff matrix.

The chicken joke is an anti-joke that morphed into an actual joke, but we've induced [jamais vu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamais_vu). Over and over again, humans teach the new humans that pointlessness is the point.

But when we search for meaning in the seemingly meaningless, we discover motor vehicles, mathematics, misery, metahumor -- much more than a mere joke. Pulling that fraying string in your underpants, you find that the entire universe unfurls and, against all odds, everything remains connected.
