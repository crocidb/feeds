Hey everyone!

This is the list of all the changes we've done to our projects during the month of June.

<img src="../media/content/videos/june_2025.jpg" alt="two humanoid animal creatures, a wolf and a dog, are working together on a turnip computer." loading="lazy" width="1200" height="679">

* **100r.ca**, updated [costs](https://100r.ca/site/costs.html), [hauling out](https://100r.ca/site/hauling_out.html), [united states](https://100r.ca/site/united_states.html) and [teapot gelcoat](https://100r.ca/site/teapot_gelcoat.html). Added [san juan islands](https://100r.ca/site/san_juan_islands.html).
* **[Rabbit Waves](https://rabbitwaves.ca)**, added all content links to the main page, released a new page called [Boatswain's Call](https://rabbitwaves.ca/site/boatswain_call.html).
* **[Orca](https://100r.ca/site/orca.html)**, embedded runtime in the [reference guide](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/orca.html).
* **[Left](https://100r.ca/site/left.html)**, made lots of little improvements and optimizations!
* **Solresol**, learnt [some profanities](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/resolsido.html) in a musical conlang?

For a few days, Pino became a [land creature](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2025/06/06.html), living on stilts, while we scrubbed and re-painted the lower part of the hull. Our propeller had a bit of a wobble, which we *hope* is now corrected. We also battled with the [old wheel quadrant](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2025/06/04.html) and were finally able to remove it, at least a part of it. Boaters have frequently helped us while we were in boatyards, and we are finally able to pay it forward. We offered both advice to those who asked and lent tools to folks that needed them. It felt nice. [Teapot](https://100r.ca/site/teapot.html)'s new bottom has seen water for the first time, the new gelcoat will allow us to take it around into bays for many more years to come.

We spent many June days working on both **Turnip Complete**(Uxn book) and the enhanced version of the [Victoria to Sitka Logbook](https://100r.ca/site/victoria_to_sitka_logbook.html), with frequent breaks to enjoy the [beautiful places](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2025/06/13.html) we found ourselves in.

The beginning of our sailing season has been very blustery, allowing for some good sailing, but also often forcing us to wait at anchor for clement weather. Later, we sailed through the [San Juan Islands](https://100r.ca/site/san_juan_islands.html) to meet up with some Merveillans on Blakely Island. We are very grateful to be part of a community of such kind, curious, and generous people. The image that was drawn for this month's update represents cooperation between members of [Merveilles](https://merveilles.town/).

**Book Club:** This month we are reading *Ill Met By Moonlight* by Sarah A. Hoyt, *Silmarillion* by J.R.R Tolkien and *Girl's Last Tour* by Tsukumizu.
