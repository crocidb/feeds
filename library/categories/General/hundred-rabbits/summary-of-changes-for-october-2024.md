Hey everyone!

This is the list of all the changes we've done to our projects during the month of October.

Summary Of Changes
----------

![little ninj has carved a pumpkin, saying BEE-HOLD! The pumpkin is smiling, in a non-threatening and eager kind of way. A bowl with pumpkin pulp and a knife are laying nearby with some fallen tree leaves scattered on the ground](https://100r.ca/media/content/videos/october_2024.jpg)

* **100r.ca**, added [Rabbit Waves](https://100r.ca/site/rabbit_waves.html) and [Logbooks](https://100r.ca/site/logbooks.html). Updated [woodstove installation](https://100r.ca/site/woodstove_installation.html#oct2024), [no windlass](https://100r.ca/site/no_windlass.html) with [1 photo](https://100r.ca/media/content/knowledge/anchoring_gloves_old.jpg), [mini dodger](https://100r.ca/site/mini_dodger.html#october2023) and [Victoria to Sitka logbook](https://100r.ca/site/victoria_to_sitka_logbook.html) with Week [3](https://100r.ca/site/victoria_to_sitka_logbook.html#week3) and [4](https://100r.ca/site/victoria_to_sitka_logbook.html#week4).

Pino is back in Victoria. Being back in a city also means that we are shipping [sticker sheets](https://100r.ca/site/store.html#stickers) again! Devine found a spirograph set at a thrift store for 5$, we now make [patterns](https://100r.ca/media/content/stream/2024_spiro.jpg) on every letter we ship.

We are happy to announce the official release of [Rabbit Waves](https://100r.ca/site/rabbit_waves.html)! The idea for the project came after discussing the disappearance of certain traditional seasteading skills and maritime communication knowledge that we believe are valuable when electronics misbehave, but that are also just generally fun to learn and use. The world of the micro-site will grow as we think of new ideas to expand it.

Devine participated in [Drawtober](https://forum.merveilles.town/thread/101/drawtober-2024-109/) again this year and completed a [zine](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/pocket_rewriting.html) that teaches the basics of multiset rewriting with examples, it also includes the source for a tiny Fractran interpreter. Since its release, many people have printed their own. Avanier went a step further and [re-drew the zine](https://100r.ca/media/content/stream/2024_joshavanier_zine.jpg) on black paper! Devine also released an [interactive version](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/tote.html), and CapitalEx created with it a [beautiful little world to explore](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5yiso80ANlc)! [Handmade Seattle 2024](https://handmadecities.com/seattle/) is coming up, Devine will be there to talk about weird computer stuff, and will hand out copies of the zine too.

This year, we carved a Calcifer [pumpkin](https://100r.ca/media/content/inventory/pumpkin_calcifer_01.jpg) (see our other [Halloween pumpkins](https://100r.ca/site/halloween_pumpkins.html)).

**Book Club:** This month we read *The Memoirs of Sherlock Holmes* by Arthur Conan Doyle.
