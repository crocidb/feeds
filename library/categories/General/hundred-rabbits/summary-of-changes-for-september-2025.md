Hey everyone!

This is the list of all the changes we've done to our projects during the month of September.

<img src="../media/content/videos/august_2025.jpg" alt="a rabbit is sitting amongst a pile of cushions, with the uxn system in one hand and the heart of the system, a beet, in the other" loading="lazy" width="1200" height="679">

* **[Noodle](../site/noodle.html)**, redesigned [the interface](../media/content/projects/noodle_15.jpg).
* **Wiki**, updated [](../site/lpg.html#troubleshoot) with a new photo.
* **[Punk Rabbits](https://kokorobot.ca/site/punk_rabbits.html)**, completed 19 new rabbits(#35-53).
* **[NanoTalk](https://git.sr.ht/~rabbits/nanotalk)**, released a Nanochat client, written in Tal.

As promised, we released a revised transcription of Devine's talk [Permacomputing 101](../site/permacomputing_101.html) for [Critical Signals 2025](https://criticalsignals.nz/)! We have moved our website to a Canadian TDL(**[100r.ca](https://100r.ca)**), so be sure to update your bookmarks! We will keep the .co version of our website online for redundancy.

Rek finished revising the upcoming version of the [Victoria to Sitka logbook](../site/victoria_to_sitka_logbook.html) and passed the text over to Devine for proof-reading. The finished publication will feature 104 drawings, 19 recipes, and 18 new sections on a variety of topics. We hope to release the digital version early next year, and the printed version a bit later.

Our friend [Erik(d6)](http://plastic-idolatry.com/erik) designed a bespoke chat server so that the [Uxn](../site/uxn.html) community could meet up through an interface designed specifically to interface nicely with tiny clients so we hacked together [a little rom](../media/content/projects/uxnchat.png) for it. Get in touch if you'd like to hang out on there!

Devine is performing in Montreal on October 11th, as Aliceffekt, at the [AMP Festival 2025](https://ampindustrialevents.com). They've also released a new album this month, called [Ver'Iystl](https://aliceffekt.bandcamp.com/album/veriytsl)(Bandcamp), adding further dimension to the many places populating the [Neauismetica](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/neauismetica.html). Let us know if you're planning to come!

**Book Club:** This month we are reading *The Glass Bead Game* by Hermann Hesse, and we are continuing to read *Middlemarch* by George Eliot.
