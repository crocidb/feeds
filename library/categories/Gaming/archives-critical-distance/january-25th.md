This Week in Videogame Blogging is a roundup highlighting the most important critical writing on games from the past seven days.

High Guard, Low Life
----------

Our opening section this week brings together the industry-level inquiries and perspectives, focusing on demographic trends (real or bullshit), indie development, and the rather selective battles the enthusiast press deigns to pick.

* [If You Ask Nicely | Autumn Wright](https://autumn-wright.com/if-you-ask-nicely/)  
  Autumn Wright weighs the priorities of criticism against those of the enthusiast press, noting the selective nature of poptimist defenses (**Further Reading:** [Kaile Hultner on Dan Stapleton’s editorial about IGN’s review policies](https://noescapevg.com/time-is-money-a-response-to-review-code/)).
* [Please, Stop Using Gen Z To Rationalise AI Slop | This Week in Videogames](https://thisweekinvideogames.com/feature/please-stop-using-gen-z-to-rationalise-ai-slop/)  
  Maddie Ange sets the record straight when AI boosters try to paint Gen Z as the slop generation (**Further Reading:** [Maddie’s previous writing on the impacts of LLMs on voice actors](https://thisweekinvideogames.com/feature/how-the-effort-and-cost-of-human-voice-actors-compares-to-generative-ai/)).
* [RETURN TO SENDER | DEEP-HELL](https://deep-hell.com/return-to-sender/)  
  Skeleton contemplates the crumbling cyberpunk edifices of a *Half-Life 2* mod and of indie games development.

>
>
> “When I returned after my brief stint back on the west coast after the holidays my relationship to G-String had changed. Rather than being something I fired up at random in the middle of the night – here it was something more real. I personally knew developers who were slowly fighting on their backs to be recognized for new work they wanted to do. Like the developer’s posts of G-String it seems like every creative I know feels backed into a corner. Either the producer in their head is asking for lightning to strike twice, or the company they have to work for to cover the costs of overheads. The hair rising tingle of florescent lights and paid-for lunches while a group of people tell you they can only afford to make sure hits.”
>
>

Any Objections, Lady?
----------

Next we’ve got a couple sections dedicated to single-game analysis. This first set scrutinizes capitalist and authoritarian power structures in the games that we play.

* [SEIZURES NOT MADE BY VIRTUE OF DUE PROCESS | killjoy](https://killjoy.bearblog.dev/metroid-samus-is-a-cop-bounty-hunter/)  
  Will Butler investigates Samus Aran’s cop history and tendencies (**Further Reading:** [Skeleton on Samus’ many contradictions over the years](https://deep-hell.com/ssamusaaran/)).
* [Ruminations on Mouthwashing | Medium](https://kat-pixeladay.medium.com/transcript-ruminations-on-mouthwashing-bd7ae03265a3)  
  Kat contemplates guilt, responsibility, and the cruelties of capitalism in *Mouthwashing* (**Further Reading:** [Franny on *Mouthwashing*](https://gamesline.net/mouthwashing-review/)).
* [Failing to Fill Space | Unwinnable](https://unwinnable.com/2026/01/23/failing-to-fill-space/)  
  Wallace Truesdale takes the measure of purposeful emptiness and loneliness in *Unbeatable* (**Further Reading:** [Maverick on *Unbeatable*](https://gamesline.net/its-a-good-sound-just-not-my-sound-unbeatable-pc-review/)).

>
>
> “The perspective is oddly grounding — to go from the high-energy rhythm game action at one of *Unbeatable*’s concerts, which gives us one of the closer views of the band, to these strolls where the world looks like it can swallow you — in that it underlines both how grand the band is becoming while also portraying how small they ultimately are. It’s as if the world itself is presenting a call to action to the band members, and subsequently the player, to push back against the world’s emptiness with the only thing that can match its vastness: music. To put it another way, an individual body could never fill an empty stadium, but the music it produces might stand a chance.”
>
>

Banjo Renewie
----------

This second set dwells more on temporality and *(quickly looks up Bakhtin)* chronotopes.

* [Rhythms of the Everyday in Animal Crossing | Eteo Archetypes](https://www.eteogames.com/rhythms-of-the-everyday-in-animal-crossing/)  
  Jay Castello takes the scenic route through the seasons in *Animal Crossing* (**Further Reading:** [Holly Nielsen on winter and death in *Friends of Mineral Town*](https://www.eteogames.com/winter-and-death-in-harvest-moon/)).
* [Playing The Plague Doctor | Stop Caring](https://www.stopcar.ing/pla-3/)  
  Artemis Octavio reflects on her third trip to the Steppe (**Further Reading:** [Grace Benfell on *Pathologic 3*](https://www.avclub.com/pathologic-3-story-criticism)).
* [Rocket science and marshmallows against toxic nihilism | Lia’s blog](https://lia-sae.net/articles/2026/01/rocket-science-and-marshmallows.html)  
  Lia dwells on *Outer Wilds* and a future not yet written.

>
>
> “There is frustration there, of course. We both know that every extra year comes with a bodycount. I can’t imagine how many friends she’s buried by now. But that doesn’t mean we give up, does it? At the very least it is always worth blunting the damage. At the same time, those small actions eventually start gently altering the trajectory we’re on.”
>
>

Logical Fal’Cie
----------

Genre section! Genre section? Are *FFXIII* and *Kingdom Hearts* in the same genre?

* [Part 1.9: The Crystarium | No Escape](https://noescapevg.com/part-1-9-the-crystarium/)  
  Kaile Hultner closes the book on *Final Fantasy XIII* and sets their sights on the rest of the fabula.
* [Experience Changes the Game – Kingdom Hearts Final Mix | Pixpen](https://pixpen.co.uk/2026/01/19/experience-changes-the-game-kingdom-hearts-final-mix/)  
  Sam Howitt returns to the original *Kingdom Hearts* after leveling up.

>
>
> “It felt good to finally finish parts of a game that had seemed impossible so long ago, alongside the comforts of childhood memories it all brings forth. The game had become significantly easier, but in a way that I had earned through effort and understanding. I didn’t beat those superbosses through brute force from my extra strength, I learned what abilities would be useful in offensive and defensive contexts and applied that.”
>
>

Full Spectrum Knight
----------

These next three picks inflect their object texts with historical context and consideration.

* [Batman – “Batcraft parts” | Super Chart Island](https://superchartisland.com/batman/)  
  Iain Mew details and situates the original ZX Spectrum Batroidvania as well as the creative duo who put it together (**Further Reading:** [more Spectrum writing from Iain](https://superchartisland.com/valhalla/)).
* [Why Does Donkey Kong Throw Barrels? | Thrilling Tales of Old Video Games](https://www.thrillingtalesofoldvideogames.com/blog/why-does-donkey-kong-throw-barrels)  
  Drew Mackie pursues a couple of different leads on where DK’s primary ordnance might have come from (**Further Reading:** [Drew on the origin of Pauline](https://www.thrillingtalesofoldvideogames.com/blog/pauline-donkey-kong-nintendos-first-leading-lady)).
* [Dead as Dreams | Bullet Points Monthly](https://bulletpointsmonthly.com/2026/01/21/dead-as-dreams-the-seance-of-blake-manor)  
  Astrid Anne Rose details the Irish history and mythology which inform *The Seance of Blake Manor*.

>
>
> “By rooting out petty grievances and musty grudges and convincing his fellow guests not to attend the séance, Ward effectively marshals facts and logic into battle against self-delusion. The conventions of the adventure game serve him well here. But Coventry can’t simply be dissuaded from his revenge; the detective mechanics are useless against him because the facts of his past, his buried trauma, are a fiction. Ward instead has to fill in the blanks around the real Coventry-Blake, winding closer to the truth of his identity and his motive by groping in the dark. Beneath Blake Manor, the tainted dreams of Goibniu radiate, soaking the manor in a false reality where the land belongs to whoever’s name is on the deed and the path of history is true and just.”
>
>

Patch Notes

* [Unfair Flips (Flowers, 2025) / Q-UP (Everybody House Games, 2025) | exp.](https://expzine.com/2026/01/13/unfair-flips-flowers-2025-q-up-everybody-house-games-2025/)
