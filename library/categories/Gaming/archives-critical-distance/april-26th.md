Welcome back readers.

This time around I’m taking a bit of a breather with a lighter week, but we’ve still got fourteen new-and-cool reads for you to check out. If you liked this technically-on-time-going-by-my-time-zone issue, check out our [Patreon](http://patreon.com/critdistance)!

This Week in Videogame Blogging is a roundup highlighting the most important critical writing on games from the past seven days.

Titanium Court
----------

Two up top this week for new game *Titanium Court*. I keep getting told to play this one.

* [The year’s weirdest game is hard to explain and even harder to put down | The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/915891/titanium-court-review-indie-game?view_token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiJ9.eyJpZCI6Imd1TkdnTXpUUTIiLCJwIjoiL2VudGVydGFpbm1lbnQvOTE1ODkxL3RpdGFuaXVtLWNvdXJ0LXJldmlldy1pbmRpZS1nYW1lIiwiZXhwIjoxNzc3Mjk4MTE0LCJpYXQiOjE3NzY4NjYxMTR9.0Jbldivzc5hpmzPEVNoQ90ayzGUcoUa2XN7cGagv2Qo)  
  Alexis Ong begins to peel back the layers of the *Titanium Court* (**Further Reading:** [Kastel on *Void Stranger*](https://kastelpls.substack.com/p/void-stranger-the-sokobans-heart)).
* [Titanium Court Makes You Reckon With Your Own Magic | Exaclaw](https://exalclaw.com/titanium-court-and-magic/)  
  Wallace Truesdale unfurls the semiotics of the Court.

>
>
> “Titanium Court has been called and marketed as hard to describe, but after hours of matching stones and water, fighting centaurs and and gunboats, and prying answers out of friendly foes and antagonistic allies, it might be better to say that the game encourages you to describe it in your own way rather than rely on storefront tags. It asks that you recognize your own magical ability and reckon with the impact it has on the worlds both in and out of the Titanium Court.”
>
>

Relay Race
----------

*Marathon* articles have mostly gotten their own sections here, but this week I think these three go really well together.

* [An Incision Tracing the Stock Market Curve — Cruelty Squad Review | Gamesline](https://gamesline.net/an-incision-tracing-the-stock-market-curve-cruelty-squad-review/)  
  Lilith assesses the aesthetics and animosity of *Cruelty Squad* (**Further Reading:** [Skeleton on *Cruelty Squad*](https://deep-hell.com/im-thinkin-arbys/)).
* [In ‘Samson,’ Victory Is Seeing The Sunrise | But Why Tho?](https://butwhytho.net/2026/04/in-samson-victory-is-seeing-the-sunrise/)  
  Will Borger revels in the thrills of the videogame crime drama (**Further Reading:** [Yussef Cole on *Mafia: The Old Country*](https://bulletpointsmonthly.com/2025/09/10/a-real-world-mafia-the-old-country)).
* [Ante Up | Bullet Points Monthly](https://bulletpointsmonthly.com/2026/04/22/ante-up-marathon)

>
> “Humanity is so alienated from itself, wrote Walter Benjamin, that it can “experience its own destruction as an aesthetic pleasure of the first order.” The runner sacrifices her mind on the altar of the machine zone; she surrenders her consciousness to hyperpop entropy. The world is at bay. Her past is cloudy. Her future is certain: run.”
>

Two for the Books
----------

This week’s interview picks both highlight underlooked histories.

* [The Second Life of Moon Child – A Conversation with Metin Seven | Gamesline](https://gamesline.net/the-second-life-of-moon-child-a-conversation-with-metin-seven/)  
  John talks to developer Metin Seven about the recently viral 90s videogame hero and his now-open-sourced game.
* [“It’s Almost Like A Religion” – The Incredible Journey To Document Nintendo’s History In Brazil | Time Extension](https://www.timeextension.com/features/its-almost-like-a-religion-the-incredible-journey-to-document-nintendos-history-in-brazil)  
  Jack Yarwood talks to Pablo Miyazawa about the Nintendistas project (**Further Reading:** [Felipe Pepe on the gentrification of videogames history](https://felipepepe.medium.com/the-gentrification-of-video-game-history-dfe11f1e08ae)).

>
>
> “”It’s something that we, the people who like Nintendo, have called ourselves for a long time here in Brazil,” says Miyazawa. “We have this suffix ‘- istas’, which is something that you can put at the end of many words. So, for people who like Sony games, we have the term Sonistas. For Xbox, we have the Caixistas, because box translates to ‘Caixa’ in Portuguese. So Nintendistas is something funny, because it means people who like Nintendo, but at the same time, it can almost be translated as someone who sees and feels Nintendo like a football team, or as something very important in their lives. It’s almost like a religion to them.””
>
>

SCPH-1001
----------

These next two picks both examine cool intersections between games and music.

* [Of Course Swansea Likes DMX | Medium](https://medium.com/@hiyeahrow/of-course-swansea-likes-dmx-d23339fdfea6)  
  Hiero de Lima cross-compares kayfabe in hip-hop and *Mouthwashing* (**Further Reading:** [Hiero’s previous writing on Swansea](https://intothespine.com/2025/11/01/hopes-dreams-and-marzipan/)).
* [LATTICE 200EC7: Haruomi Hosono’s hidden video game soundtrack | once bitten, twice shy](https://www.shy.center/p/lattice-200ec7)  
  Shy chronicles a long history of creative exchange between videogames and the alumni of Yellow Magic Orchestra (**Further Reading:** [Misty de Méo on *Moon Light Café*](https://cdrom.ca/music/multimedia/2025/07/01/moon-light-cafe.html)).

>
>
> “It feels appropriate that the first person to take the initiative to present video game music as something worth considering as its own piece of art would make video game music that could be appreciated both in and out of context with the work that accompanied it. The evolution of technology that inspired Hosono and his friends to make music that sounded like the future enabled him to create something that embodies the respect he shared for video games and music equally.”
>
>
