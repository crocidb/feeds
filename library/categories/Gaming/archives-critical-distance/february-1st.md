This Week in Videogame Blogging is a roundup highlighting the most important critical writing on games from the past seven days.

Playing with Power
----------

This week let’s start with some critical analyses of the games industry past and present.

* [Rad Dudes, Rude ‘Tude, and the Family, Too: How Nintendo and Sega Advertisements Shaped the Image of Gaming in the 1990s | ACM Digital Library](https://dl.acm.org/doi/epdf/10.1145/3793255)  
  Megan Condis and Jess Morrissette close the image gap between the 90s advertising of Sega and Nintendo as each vied to shape the “gamer” as a cultural identity (**Further Reading:** [Previous writing from Jess on game adverts and gaming culture](https://www.pcgamer.com/lara-croft-the-art-of-virtual-seduction-is-the-ultimate-cringey-relic-of-late-90s-game-advertising/)).
* [The Gaming Time Loop | Inner Spiral](https://innerspiral.lol/Blog/timeloop/timeloop)  
  Alli unpacks the remake trend in mainstream games as symptomatic of an industry unwilling to write new history for itself (**Further Reading:** [Ashley Schofield on *FFVII Remake*](https://ashlikeadragon.bearblog.dev/beyond-the-steel-sky/)).

>
>
> “We are essentially watching the mainstream video game industry transform from a creative powerhouse into a high-end museum gift shop, where the primary business model is selling us souvenirs of experiences we had twenty years ago rather than creating new memories. The tragedy isn’t that these remakes are bad games, as I said before, many of them are technically brilliant productions; but that their dominance signals a complete lack of faith in the future of the medium. The industry has become so efficient at monetizing our past that it has forgotten how to invest in its own future, creating a feedback loop that feels increasingly claustrophobic.”
>
>

Patch Notes
----------

Our interview picks this week highlight subversive genres and marginalized creators.

* [How Do You Make A Citizen Sleeper-Like? Also, What Even Makes Something A [Game You Know Here]-Like? | Exalclaw](https://exalclaw.com/2026/01/29/citizen-sleeper-like-duskpunk/)  
  Wallace Truesdale chats with James Patton about what makes *Dustpunk* a Sleeper-like but also a Sleeper-unlike (**Further Reading:** [Ogre Run on *Clair Obscur* and the limits of genre labels](https://ogrerun.com/2025/12/14/anime-rpg-trash-or-clair-obscur-and-genre-trouble/)).
* [Spirit Swap developer Alex Abou Karam on marketing a game in times of war | Mothership](https://www.mothership.blog/soft-not-weak-spirit-swap-development/)  
  Zoë Hannah chats with Alex Abou Karam about organizing support and aid for Palestinians while also trying to get their game launched (**Further Reading:** [Amaar Chowdhury’s interview with the devs behind *Palestine Skating Game*](https://www.huckmag.com/article/palestine-skating-game-rollerskating-resistance-video-development-graffiti)).
* [How I’m Reimagining the Haitian Revolution in This Video Game | Killscreen](https://www.killscreen.com/dom-rabrun-veve-punk-haitian-revolution-videogame/)  
  Jamin Warren talks to Dom Rabrun about making a game that approaches the Haitian Revolution differently (**Further Reading:** [Ade Adeniji’s interview with Abubakar Salim on *Tales of Kenzera: ZAU*](https://store.epicgames.com/en-US/news/abubakar-salim-talks-his-first-game-tales-of-kenzera-zau-an-afrofuturist-platformer)).

>
>
> “”I’m not interested in showing the murder,” he says. “I’m interested in what that dialogue tree looks like.” Saint-Domingue had 16 racial classifications. Navigating that social structure without a weapon—only words, only choices, only the consequences of saying the wrong thing to the wrong person—that’s the revolution Rabrun wants to put in your hands.”
>
>

Pale Fire
----------

We’ve got a couple genre-specific pairings coming up next. The first takes the long view on RPGs and history.

* [7: Molmorth no Kihetai and the End of Childhood | Ogre Run](https://ogrerun.com/2026/01/08/7-molmorth-no-kihetai-and-the-end-of-childhood/)  
  Ogre Run unpacks a Japan-only millennial RPG with a real doozy of a generational postgame.
* [Hauntology beyond the Pale. Searching for anarchism in Disco Elysium | flocksy takes](https://www.flocksy.de/hauntology-beyond-the-pale-searching-for-anarchism-in-disco-elysium.html)  
  alex takes lessons from a world given over to nihilism into a world that is not (**Further Reading:** [Emily Price on *Disco Elysium*](https://post45.org/2024/04/further-and-further-away/)).

>
>
> “This is why the Pale would less than 30 years after the events of Disco Elysium ultimately succeed and cover the entirety of its world. The past finally erasing the present and future. For unlike our own world, Elysium only has failed ideologies and a nihilist belief in the status quo. But we might still have a chance. Not by embracing anarchism but by embracing what anarchists have always stood for: a future for everyone, a future where no-one is lower or higher than the next and no-one goes hungry. Look around and you will find – amidst this doom and evil monarchs in high places – myriads of groups of all kinds of stripes already working on bringing this future about. A rainbow of good created in this world right now.”
>
>

Carrying Capacity
----------

Now let’s look at some horror games.

* [Resident Evil Village: A bit mouldy | Kimimi The Game-Eating She-Monster](https://kimimithegameeatingshemonster.com/2026/01/26/resident-evil-village-a-bit-mouldy/)  
  Kimimi shares detailed play notes on the (soon to be supplanted) most recent mainline *Resident Evil*, finding an enjoyable but noncommittal game (**Further Reading:** [Emma Kostopolus on *RE Village*](https://bulletpointsmonthly.com/2021/06/16/snake-eating-its-tail-resident-evil-village)).
* [Fascist violence reaped domestic tragedy in The Medium | AV Club](https://www.avclub.com/medium-bloober-team-silent-hill)  
  Madeline Blondeau revisits *The Medium* and the thematic purpose of its contentious portrayal of child sexual abuse (**Further Reading:** [Ashley Bardhan on *Silent Hill f*](https://plant-or-beast.net/posts/2025-10-19-Lovers-meeting)).

>
>
> “To rationalize and explain an action is not to justify it, though, and *The Medium* is careful to not excuse Richard from the weight of his sins. Rather, it uses his sexual dysfunction as an overt critique of fascist ideology. On a subtextual level, the writers argue Nazism cuts psychological, emotional wounds into the psyche of anyone touched by its evil. This litigates fascism as a stunted ideology—gazing to the past at the expense of harm done to the present and doom sowed for the future.”
>
>

By Design
----------

Let’s shift gears and look at some reflections on design as both formalism and lived practice.

* [“I fit here”; a puzzle game love letter | Gamesline](https://gamesline.net/i-fit-here-a-puzzle-game-love-letter-by-penny/)  
  Penny thinks through the play in design and the design in play when it comes to puzzle games.
* [Whispered Poetry | Bullet Points Monthly](https://bulletpointsmonthly.com/2026/01/28/whispered-poetry-many-nights-a-whisper)  
  Gareth Damian Martin ponders a poetics of games that make you feel with rhythm and ritual (**Further Reading:** [Robin Bea on *Many Nights a Whisper*](https://www.inverse.com/gaming/many-nights-a-whisper-steam-pc-release)).
* [FAFO | Bloomed Wings Blog](https://bloomedwings.com/2026/01/23/fafo/)  
  Cind explores a fuck-around-and-find-out theory of design, play, speedrunning, and community resilience (**Further Reading:** [Madison Schmalzer on speedrunning *Undertale* and transition](https://www.polygon.com/22392356/undertale-speedrun-trans-glitches-gender/)).

>
>
> “I watch my kid on the playground with some kids he has never known, yet suddenly they all understand the idea of tag. Or hide-and-seek. We meet up with one of his friends, and as her mom and I sit on the bench and watch, we laugh at how they ping off each other, one pitching an idea for some imaginary castle or other that they’re storming, and then the other adds on and changes the game, two youngsters just finding out what fun they can have. Can she teach him to do the monkey bars? Can he help her climb up the slide? The games keep coming, flashing into youthful minds and disappearing into the cold air when it’s time to go.”
>
>

Mythopoesis
----------

These next two selections each examine intersections between games and myth.

* [Angeline Era vs Videogames | Unwinnable](https://unwinnable.com/2026/01/29/angeline-era-vs-videogames/)  
  Emily Price dwells on dreams, faith, and mythology in *Angeline Era* (**Further Reading:** [Melos Han-Tani on *Angeline Era*‘s combat design](https://buttondown.com/melodicambient/archive/27-tips-for-future-bumpslash-designers/)).
* [Tithonus’ Drone: Myth, Loss and Memory in Phoenix Springs | Unwinnable](https://unwinnable.com/2026/01/27/tithonus-drone-myth-loss-and-memory-in-phoenix-springs/)  
  KM Nelson traces the steps of the endless cycle in *Phoenix Springs*.

>
>
> “Iris is also ensconced in the sanctum of a stasis pod. While her cycle of rebirth within the simulacrum resembles the biological processes of the cicada, she is not the Tithonus transformed by Eos, but the Crying One shut behind a set of shining doors: endlessly babbling, endlessly degrading. She is forever cocooned, with no promise of escape or respite, disconnected from both the world at large and the others interred alongside her.”
>
>

Skate or Climb
----------

Here are some more single-game pieces exploring labour, locality, and lines.

* [Ruminations on Skate Story | No Escape](https://noescapevg.com/ruminations-on-skate-story/)  
  Kaile Hultner chronicles the agony and awe of finally nailing the line (**Further Reading:** [Alexis Ong on *Skate Story*](https://www.eurogamer.net/skate-story-review)).
* [Cairn Review | IGN](https://www.ign.com/articles/cairn-review)  
  Will Borger climbs the mountain (**Further Reading:** [Emily Durham on *Celeste*](https://sidequest.zone/2020/03/17/climbing-mount-celeste/)).
* [Wilmot’s Warehouse is watching me, and laughing | Nyapolitan Bazaar](https://nyapolitan.wordpress.com/2026/01/26/wilmots-warehouse-is-watching-me-and-laughing/)  
  Brea sorts through the wide interpretative space of *Wilmot’s Warehouse*.
* [Home is a Black Hole | Norco (2022)](https://undermatrixjunction.blogspot.com/2026/01/home-is-black-hole-norco-2022.html)  
  Roxanne B. ruminates on *Norco*, the ghosts of the old world, and her own experiences in the South (**Further Reading:** [Lotus on *Norco*](https://lotusrootrecords.neocities.org/writing/posts/2022-09-15-notes-on-norco)).

>
>
> “I don’t know if I can call the South home. I only lived there for a few years, and I barely experienced it. I think what mostly gets me is that fact, that I only saw a glimpse of what it had to offer, the lost promises and life on my own. How can I call a place home when I only skimmed the surface of what composes it. Transience cannot replace homecoming.”
>
>

Critical Chaser
----------

Our closing section this week is bone dry. Forgive me.

* [Why do video game skeletons put themselves back together? | AV Club](https://www.avclub.com/regenerating-skeletons-games)  
  Bee Wertheimer has a bone to pick with gaming’s ubiquitous fascination with animate, regenerating skeletons.

>
>
> “There is something beautiful about how some of the most eternal and unifying cultural phenomena derive from great disaster. In the face of unspeakable tragedy, when the flame of humanity flickers at its weakest, we turn to creation for salvation. And then those creations inspire more, spanning generations and centuries, influencing humanity throughout the world and across the ages. Art and culture unite us in our suffering, and, in spite of everything, we survive—like video game skeletons.”
>
>
